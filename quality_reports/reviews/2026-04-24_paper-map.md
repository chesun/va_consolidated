### Methods (section 3)

| Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Estimator | CFR-style "value-added with drift", adapted from teacher VA to school VA |
| Equation 1 | Y_ist = phi0 + phi1 X_ist + gamma_t + lambda_st + xi_st + epsilon_ist -- residual collapsed to school-year, then drift-shrunk by IV-like prediction from other-year residuals |
| Note | The paper does **NOT** include school FE in equation (1) -- footnote says estimates with school FE correlate at 0.99 with the no-FE estimates used in the paper. This differs from the CFR baseline and the Naven dissertation. See line 233. |
| Equation 2 | Pass-through model: Y_ist = alpha_0 + alpha_1 X_ist + gamma_t + rho lambda_st + beta_st + theta_st + e_ist. Parameter of interest: rho. |
| Equation 3 | School-level mechanism regression: lambda_s = phi0 + phi1 X_s + phi2 Index_s + epsilon_s |

### v1 vs v2 prior-score variants (NOT in paper text -- from MEMORY.md)

The paper uses **v1**. v2 is an alternative robustness specification with different prior-score grade/year combinations. Per `create_prior_scores_v1.doh` / `create_prior_scores_v2.doh`:

| Cohort | v1 ELA prior | v1 Math prior | v2 ELA prior | v2 Math prior |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| spring-2015 | 8th ELA (sp 2012) | 6th math (sp 2010) | 6th ELA (sp 2012) | 6th math (sp 2010) |
| spring-2016 | 8th ELA (sp 2013) | 6th math (sp 2011) | 6th ELA (sp 2013) | 6th math (sp 2011) |
| spring-2017 | 7th ELA (sp 2013) | 6th math (sp 2012) | 6th ELA (sp 2013) | 6th math (sp 2012) |
| spring-2018 | 8th ELA (sp 2015) | 8th math (sp 2015) | 8th ELA (sp 2015) | 8th math (sp 2015) |

The 7th-grade-ELA cohort exception in v1 (spring 2017) is because California did not administer tests in 2013-14 during the SBAC transition.

### Validity tests

- **Specification test:** regress u_ist on lambda_st-hat, null is coefficient = 1 (Table 2/3 row 2).
- **Forecast bias tests** (Table 2/3 rows 3-6):

## 5. Survey Indices (Mechanisms section)

**Data source:** CalSCHLS (California School Climate, Health, and Learning Surveys), waves 2017-2019. Cannot be linked to CDE individual records -- analysis is school-level only.

**Three indices** with question wording in Online Appendix B:

- **School Climate** (Cronbach's alpha 0.94, ~20 questions across parents/students/staff)
- **Teacher and Staff Quality** (alpha 0.90, ~17 questions)
- **Counseling Support** (alpha 0.66, 4 questions)

Construction: school-level averages of standardized response variables, then index averaged across questions, then standardized to SD = 1.

**Equation:** lambda_s = phi0 + phi1 X_s + phi2 Index_s + epsilon_s where X_s is means of base VA controls.

**Output table:** `imputed_index_combined_wdemo.tex`. The "imputed" prefix implies missing-survey-response imputation feeds into this table -- consistent with caschls/file_dict.md mention of `imputation.do` and `imputedcategoryindex.do` under `share/factoranalysis/`. To be confirmed in deep-read.

## 6. Implications for the consolidation work

What this paper map tells us about the load-bearing scripts that must reproduce in the consolidated repo:

1. **VA estimation pipeline** (must produce):
   - Base and restricted samples (sample-creation script)
   - Prior-score creation under v1 variant (`create_prior_scores_v1.doh`)
   - Equation (1) residuals + drift-shrinkage by school for 4 outcomes (ELA, math, enr-2yr, enr-4yr)
   - 5 control specifications per outcome
   - Standard deviation, specification-test coefficient, forecast-bias coefficients

2. **Validity / heterogeneity / pass-through pipeline** (must produce):
   - Tables 2-7 listed above
   - Figures 1-6 listed above
   - Including Variance-decomposition table (Eq 2 + variance ratios)

3. **Sibling crosswalk** (lives in caschls per user; feeds VA): must be available before VA estimation, since "sibling college-going" is in column 5 controls.

4. **Survey/CalSCHLS pipeline** (must produce Table 8):
   - Three indices with imputation
   - School-year averaging 2017-2019
   - Merge with VA estimates at school level
   - Note: `caschls/file_dict.md` mentions the relevant intermediate output dirs (`share/factoranalysis/`, `share/factoranalysis/imputation.do`, `share/svyvaregs/`).

5. **Geocoding / ACS** pipeline: address-level Census-tract match + nearest college distance. Address data only available 2002-03 through 2012-13.

6. **NSC linkage**: provides 2yr/4yr enrollment outcomes within 2 years of 11th grade.

These six pipelines are the canonical units of consolidation. Each gets one or more ADRs in `decisions/` once we've mapped the existing scripts that implement them.

## 7. Open questions surfaced by paper read

These are things the paper alludes to but doesn't pin down -- to resolve during deep-read or by asking the user:

- **Sibling matching specifics:** "shared address + surname in a given year, then transitive closure" -- but what about families that move? remarriages (surname change)? data-entry errors? What's the precision/recall of the match? Is there a sensitivity-analysis script?
- **NSC linkage:** by name + DOB + school? What's the match rate (paper says "slightly smaller than the test-score sample")?
- **Geocoding service / vintage:** what was used, when, what's the match rate?
- **Distance computation:** Euclidean (paper says "linear distance")? What's the list of public 2-yr and 4-yr colleges considered?
- **Standard errors:** paper consistently says "clustered at the school level" -- need to verify in code.
- **Drift / shrinkage estimation:** "predicted value from regression of u_st on u_st'" -- is this implemented as Stata's `vam` package by Stepner/Jepsen? Custom code? CFR's published `vam` ado?
- **Bibliography file:** does `literature/bibtex/common_core_va.bib` exist in the va_paper_clone repo, the fork, or caschls?
